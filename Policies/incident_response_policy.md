# Incident Response Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a structured process for identifying, responding to, and recovering from cybersecurity incidents.  
This ensures that security events are handled efficiently and that business operations can be restored quickly.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and systems that interact with the organization's information systems.

---

## Definition of a Security Incident

A security incident is any event that may compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of company systems or data.

Examples include:

- Unauthorized system access
- Malware infections
- Data breaches
- Phishing attacks
- Denial of service attacks

---

## Incident Response Process

The organization follows a structured incident response process consisting of the following stages:

### 1. Identification

Security incidents are identified through monitoring systems, security alerts, or user reports.

### 2. Containment

Steps are taken to limit the spread or impact of the incident. This may include isolating affected systems.

### 3. Eradication

The root cause of the incident is removed, such as deleting malware or patching vulnerabilities.

### 4. Recovery

Systems are restored to normal operation and monitored for further suspicious activity.

### 5. Lessons Learned

After the incident is resolved, the organization reviews the event to improve security controls and response procedures.

---

## Reporting Procedures

All suspected security incidents must be reported immediately to the security team or designated incident response contact.

Employees should report:

- Suspicious emails
- Unusual system behavior
- Unauthorized access attempts

---

## Responsibilities

### Employees

- Report suspected security incidents immediately.
- Follow security best practices.

### Security Team

- Investigate incidents
- Coordinate response actions
- Document and review incidents

---

## Policy Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action and potential termination of system access.
